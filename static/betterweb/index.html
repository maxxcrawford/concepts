Frequently Asked Questions

What is it?

What exactly is Firefox Better Web?

Firefox Better Web is a desktop extension. It combines:


1) Firefox Enhanced Tracking Protection (ETP) technology is used to block ads, trackers, fingerprinters—in other words, you get a mostly ad-free experience while being protected online. ETP works only on the Firefox desktop browser.


2) Scroll collaborates with publishers in its network to create ad-free versions of their content. It also offers audio for articles within the publisher network, and it keeps track of your reading activity across devices and apps so that you can refer to your history and see how your reading activity directly benefits the publishers you patronize. Scroll pays its publishers based on reading activity, and this amounts to over 40% more than what they would have received through ad revenue.


All of this supports the possibility of an Internet that’s not dominated by ads and ad-driven revenue. It leads the way to quality online content that you’re able to find quickly. It paves the way toward a fast, clean, and private online experience—for all.

What is a tracker? Why should it matter to me that Firefox Better Web is blocking them?

A tracker is something that companies place into websites to gather very detailed information about you, often without you realizing it. That information could include your exact location at the moment, your financial situation, health concerns, and much more.


Companies sell the extensive data that trackers collect about you to others that might want to influence your behavior. For example, they might want you to purchase their products, to influence your political beliefs, or to show you a higher airline ticket price than your neighbor.


This mass collection of personal information on the Internet greatly increases the risk that your data will be misused or leaked, and that organizations you have never even heard of will know startlingly intimate details about you and your family.


By default, Firefox has Enhanced Tracking Protection (ETP) built in to automatically prevent the collection of your personal data without your consent. It blocks a wide range of trackers, from common “everyday” trackers to more sophisticated data mining techniques. Read more about online tracking in this Mozilla article, “What you need to know about online tracking (and how to stop it).”

What do you mean by blocks “most ads”? Which ads are not blocked, and why?

Firefox’s ETP technology blocks ads from any identified tracking domains. It is possible that some ads will still be shown if the sites they’re on have not been identified as a tracking site. Firefox uses Disconnect’s tracking protections lists as a guide; ETP blocks ads from the domains on these lists, with the exception of their content list.

What do you mean that this is in beta?

Firefox Better Web is in beta, which means that the product is not fully developed and scaled. During this stage, we intend to learn better about whether and how to develop Firefox Better Web into a full-fledged product.


We need to confirm that Firefox Better Web can address a complex, pervasive problem that all users experience: The current ad revenue model de-incentivizes quality content creation while creating a hostile experience for users. Through the beta, we intend to learn from you about how to better deliver the web experience you want. We’ll also confirm that we’re enabling partner sites to create more quality content.


You can help us do all this by signing up for the beta and giving us feedback through surveys.


How it works

Does it work on all websites? What about my apps and devices?

Firefox Better Web works on all websites when you’re on the Firefox desktop browser. The Scroll-specific features—syncing, audio, and reading lists, as well as publisher direct payout—work only on partner publisher sites. To use Scroll features on all your browsers, devices and apps, use set-up links or download the Scroll app, depending on where you read. Learn more about Scroll set-up.

What are the reading tools and how do I use them?

Reading tools include audio for articles, saving articles to your reading list, and syncing your activity across devices, apps, and browsers.


Audio for articles: When you’re on an article page within one of our partner sites, you’ll see the Scroll bar at the bottom of your page. Just press the audio icon and audio will begin or resume.


Saving articles: When you’re on an article page within one of our partner sites, just go to article > click Scroll icon > click bookmark icon. You can quickly access your reading list within your Scroll apps, or just by going to scroll.com when you’re signed in.


Syncing your activity: Your reading and listening activity is automatically synced across any devices, apps, and browsers that you’ve signed into.

Will Firefox Better Web work with my current ad-blocker?

Yes, Firefox Better Web will work with your current ad-blocker. For some ad-blockers, you may need to adjust settings to ensure it is not interfering with Scroll.

Privacy

Does Scroll work with my browser’s privacy settings?

Scroll does work with a browser’s privacy settings, but sometimes you may need to manually allow “third-party cookies” from Scroll. Cookies from Scroll are used to give you the ad-free experience on partner site and to allocate your money to the sites you visit. Data is only collected on partner sites and when the scroll bar is present.

Does Firefox or Scroll sell my data?

No, neither Firefox nor Scroll sell your data.


While using Firefox Better Web you are protected by the Firefox Personal Data Promise, except when you are on Scroll publisher network sites. Scroll measures the engaged time spent with that site to calculate how much that site should earn each month. All collected data collected by Scroll can be managed on scroll.com. You can read about how Scroll looks after your personal data in the Scroll Privacy Policy.
